# Design Context — our-space

**Product.** An interactive map of Manhattan that estimates how much of a walking
route is "watched" by the cameras and sensing devices entering public space
(fixed CCTV, ACE bus cameras, dashcams, smart glasses), plus citywide exposure
and a neighborhood-diversity equity overlay. Bevy/Rust → WASM/WebGPU; a DOM
chrome wraps the Bevy canvas with an in-canvas egui control panel.

**Audience.** The general public and civically-minded New Yorkers (non-experts),
plus researchers/advocates. It is a public-dissemination tool, not a dashboard.

**Use cases.** (1) Enter a walk A→B and feel how surveilled it is; (2) explore
the citywide exposure heatmap; (3) read the diversity↔camera equity story.

**Tone / personality — the core of the brief.** EARTHY, ORGANIC, warm, human,
calm, grounded, honest, non-alarmist — held in **stark contrast** to the cold,
clinical surveillance it depicts. The interface *is* the human/natural world
(paper, soil, moss, ink); surveillance is the cold intrusion that pierces it.
Numbers are honest estimates with confidence tiers, never fear-mongering.

**Contrast strategy.** Base map + chrome = warm, organic, hand-drawn-cartography
calm. Surveillance layers (cameras, exposure heat, ACE corridors) = cold, hard,
desaturated-to-alarm accents that visibly *don't belong* — the tension is the
message.

**Constraints.** WebGPU/WASM; egui (in-canvas) + CSS (DOM chrome) + Bevy mesh
vertex/material colors must share one palette. Route stays client-side. Large
map → performance matters. Accessible, legible at a glance.

**Differentiation.** The felt contrast — a warm, almost botanical cartographic
calm pierced by cold surveillance markers. Not another dark-mode neon dashboard.
